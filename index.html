Why Create?

I’ve always been drawn to creating. When I was younger, that meant sketching and experimenting with art, eventually teaching at an art academy where I learned to think more intentionally about design, detail, and how people interact with what they create.

Over time, that curiosity shifted from how things look to how they work. I became more interested in breaking systems down, understanding how they function, and building them back in a more thoughtful way. That mindset led me to Electrical Engineering at Georgia Tech, where I now focus on biotechnology and sensing, working on systems that capture and interpret real-world signals.

I’m especially interested in developing technologies that operate outside controlled environments—whether that’s in medical applications or exploration contexts like space or environmental sensing. I’m drawn to problems where systems need to interact directly with the real world, and where engineering can make those interactions more meaningful, reliable, and useful.

At the core, I create because I want to understand the world more deeply and have a hand in shaping how we interact with it. Creating is how I test ideas, challenge assumptions, and turn curiosity into something tangible. It’s not just about building something that works—it’s about building something that makes sense, something that feels intentional, and something that can exist meaningfully in the real world.

Career Goals

Develop Experience in Bioengineering

I aim to build a strong foundation in bioengineering by working on technologies that interact with biological systems, particularly in medical and health-related applications. Gaining experience in areas like signal processing and wearable sensing will help me better understand how to capture and interpret physiological data. Through internships and hands-on projects, I want to contribute to systems that improve real-world care and monitoring.

Build Systems for Sensing & Exploration

I’m interested in designing systems that operate in dynamic, real-world environments, where sensing and responsiveness are critical. This includes applications in robotics, environmental monitoring, and potentially space exploration. Developing skills in embedded systems, signal processing, and system integration will allow me to build technologies that can reliably interact with complex environments.

Combine Creativity with Engineering

With a background in art, I approach engineering with an emphasis on design, detail, and user experience. I want to continue developing this perspective by working on projects that balance functionality with thoughtful design. Bringing a creative mindset into technical environments allows me to approach problems from a different angle and build more intuitive, human-centered systems.

Make a Meaningful Impact

Long term, I want to work on technologies that extend beyond controlled settings and have a direct impact on how we interact with the world—whether through improving medical systems or enabling exploration in challenging environments. To get there, I plan to continue building hands-on experience, learning from interdisciplinary teams, and pushing myself to take on increasingly complex problems.

Discovery Project: Smart Energy Roadmap Toolkit

This portfolio includes my Discovery Project as a central technical availability demonstration. I built a web-based toolkit that connects sensor data to an energy optimization engine. The project objective was to make real-time power profiles accessible to non-technical users while preserving high-performance computation.

The system architecture uses a Raspberry Pi gateway connected with custom PCB sensor nodes, with a backend in Python that adapts control commands using predictive modeling. I conducted user tests with 12 participants and applied iterative design changes, reducing a key usability metric (task completion time) by 28%.

Highlights: embedded sensor integration, ML-driven charge prediction, responsive UI in React, strong documentation, and a maturity plan for adding more sensors and cloud analytics leading to production readiness.
